pomme dorée

Pomme dorée!! Wait, isn't it 'pomodoro' though?

So, yes, 'pomodoro' literally means 'tomato' in Italian. However, what fun is it to build a website for this technique of spaced, timed sessions of focus without a, presumably, witty word play?

As I, Phillip Gallas, also speak french among other languages, I swear I am not trying to flex, (il faut que je dis plutôt que je suis en train d'apprendre la langue encore), I decided to search for a mixture of words that sound similar, hence 'la pomme dorée', the golden apple, came to be!

I also like playing Minecraft sometimes, and the golden apple in the game came in handy for the logo.

And how to use the website?

The page loads with a default set of pomodoro (here called 'pomme') time session. You can simply click 'Play' and use it. But if you need a custom timer, click 'Settings' and set the pomme to fit your task. Click 'Ok' and 'Play'.

Once the system is counting down, you can choose to 'Pause', 'Skip' or 'Stop' the pomme at your will. Once you run out of time, an alarm sound will pop up to let you know your pomme's up from whatever page you might be in.

Now, have fun doing your stuff with pomme dorée!
